Westboro or Barrhaven?
How to Choose Your Ottawa Neighbourhood

Two of Ottawa's most popular areas, compared honestly across price, lifestyle, commute, schools, and what you are actually getting for your money at each end of the spectrum.

Why This Comparison Comes Up So Often

Westboro and Barrhaven represent two genuinely different answers to the question of how to live in Ottawa. They attract different buyers, offer different trade-offs, and behave differently in the real estate market. The comparison comes up constantly because buyers who can afford Barrhaven at the lower end of their budget can sometimes stretch to entry-level Westboro, and vice versa. Understanding the real difference between these areas makes that trade-off decision much clearer.

Westboro: What You Are Actually Buying

Westboro's appeal is primarily about lifestyle infrastructure. Within a 10-minute walk of a typical Westboro home, you can reach the Ottawa River path, two LRT stations, Richmond Road's independent commercial strip (wine bars, specialty grocers, independent fitness studios, coffee roasters), and the community parks and beach that define the neighbourhood's outdoor culture.

The housing stock is largely older, century homes and 1940s–1960s builds on the side streets, with a growing supply of modern infill duplexes and boutique condo mid-rises along the main corridors. This means buyers are often buying a combination of location and heritage character rather than turn-key modern homes. Renovation needs are common; heating and insulation costs on older stock are higher than in newer construction.

  • Average detached price: $850K–$1.4M depending on size and renovation quality
  • Condo/stacked options available from ~$450K–$700K along Scott Street
  • LRT (Westboro and Dominion stations) makes downtown Ottawa genuinely walkable commute territory
  • Very limited supply, the neighbourhood is geographically constrained and rarely builds new
  • Ottawa River access and Westboro Beach within easy walking distance
The Westboro Price Premium

Westboro commands a premium over comparable square footage in most other Ottawa neighbourhoods, because buyers are not just paying for the house, they are paying for the 10-minute walk to everything. That premium has been consistent and has compounded meaningfully over the past decade.

Barrhaven: What You Are Actually Buying

Barrhaven's appeal is primarily about value and lifestyle stage. For a family that needs three or four bedrooms, a finished basement, a backyard for children, and a neighbourhood where the school network is dense and the streets are quiet, Barrhaven delivers more physical space per dollar than anywhere else in Ottawa at this scale.

Most of Barrhaven was built between the 1990s and the present, which means buyers are largely getting newer construction with modern electrical, plumbing, and insulation standards. The maintenance demands on a 2005-built Barrhaven detached are meaningfully lower than on a 1940s Westboro semi. The trade-off is the commute, driving to downtown from Barrhaven takes 25–40 minutes depending on time of day and route, and the neighbourhood is car-dependent for most daily needs.

  • Average detached price: $580K–$850K depending on subdivision and size
  • Executive detached in Stonebridge and Half Moon Bay: $800K–$1.1M
  • Commute by car: Queensway to downtown in 25–35 minutes off-peak
  • Strong school network: Catholic and public, both French and English
  • Large commercial node at Strandherd/Greenbank for day-to-day needs
The Barrhaven Value Case

For buyers with families or those who work remotely full-time, Barrhaven often makes more financial sense than Westboro, you get significantly more space, newer construction, and a stronger school network for substantially less money. The commute premium is real but manageable for the right buyer.

Schools

Both neighbourhoods have solid school networks. Barrhaven has a particular strength in school density, Catholic and public, French and English programs are all well-represented, and most family areas are within walking distance of multiple options. Westboro has excellent schools within its catchment (including Broadview Public School and Elmdale Public School, both highly regarded), but the network is smaller given the neighbourhood's size and residential density.

If school quality and choice is a primary decision factor, Barrhaven's sheer network size gives it a slight edge. If walkability to school is the priority, children walking to and from school safely, both areas score well.

Investment and Resale

Both areas have strong resale markets, but in different ways. Westboro's appreciation has been consistent and above the Ottawa average, driven by supply constraints and persistent demand from a buyer profile that is not going away. The neighbourhood's geographic limits mean inventory is structurally low, which keeps values firm.

Barrhaven appreciates more moderately but reliably. It is Ottawa's largest suburban community, which gives it depth and liquidity when selling, there are always buyers in the market for well-maintained Barrhaven family homes. It is not a high-speculation market, but it is a dependable one.

For investors considering rental demand: Westboro commands Ottawa's highest residential rents per square foot, driven by proximity to transit and the employment corridor. Barrhaven rentals perform well for family tenants seeking school catchments, but rental demand is more seasonal and family-specific.

The Honest Verdict

Choose Westboro if: you do not have or do not need a car for daily life, you value walkability and urban character above square footage, and your budget allows entry into the $850K+ range. The neighbourhood will continue to appreciate as Ottawa's transit network matures, and the lifestyle it offers is genuinely difficult to replicate elsewhere in the city.

Choose Barrhaven if: you have or are planning a family, your commute is manageable or you work remotely, and your budget stretches further in space than it does in location premium. The neighbourhood consistently delivers strong value for what it costs, and the family infrastructure around it, schools, parks, arenas, community centres, is among Ottawa's best.

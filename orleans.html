The Neighbourhood

Ottawa's Bilingual Heart, East of the River.

Orleans is east Ottawa's largest community and home to one of Canada's most visible francophone populations outside Quebec. Over half the neighbourhood's residents are francophone or bilingual, and that cultural depth shapes everything from the school networks to the businesses that line St. Joseph Boulevard. It is not just a suburb; it is a community with a distinct identity.

For families, Orleans delivers a combination that is hard to find elsewhere in Ottawa at this price point: newer detached homes with basements and backyards, an outstanding French and English school network, and a pace of life that prioritises community over congestion. Place d'Orléans mall, multiple arenas, and growing commercial strips along Innes Road round out the day-to-day convenience.

Highway 174 provides direct access to downtown Ottawa, and an eastern extension of the LRT Confederation Line has been discussed as a long-term city priority. Buyers entering Orleans now are getting ahead of that potential infrastructure story at a price point that still makes financial sense.

What to Expect

Living in Orleans

Three characteristics that define Orleans for buyers considering the east Ottawa market.

Bilingual Schools

Orleans has one of Ottawa's most complete French-language school networks, CEPEO Catholic and CECCE boards operate alongside English public and Catholic schools. Families choosing Orleans for schooling have genuine choice that is rare in comparable-priced suburban markets.

Value for Families

Orleans consistently offers the most detached home per dollar in Ottawa. A family can purchase a 3-bedroom detached with a yard for significantly less than equivalent properties in Barrhaven, Kanata, or central Ottawa. That affordability gap has been narrowing, making Orleans an increasingly attractive buy-now proposition.

Community Character

Orleans has its own character, not a generic suburb but a community with a strong local culture, established festivals, and a sense of belonging that newcomers often note. The mix of established families and newer arrivals creates a stable, welcoming foundation that supports long-term residential investment.

The Neighbourhood

Ottawa's Most Sought-After Address.

The Glebe occupies a privileged position in Ottawa's urban core, south of Dow's Lake and the Rideau Canal, north of Queensway, and centred on Bank Street's independent commercial strip. It is one of the few central Ottawa neighbourhoods where you can genuinely walk to everything: the canal, a farmers market, cinema, independent restaurants, hardware store, and doctor's office within four blocks of most front doors.

The housing stock reflects its age and desirability: Victorian semi-detached homes on deep narrow lots, substantial detached properties on the quiet side streets north of Fifth Avenue, and newer boutique condominiums along the canal edge. Renovated Glebe homes consistently command the city's highest price-per-square-foot for residential properties outside of a handful of Rockcliffe Park estates.

Lansdowne Park, anchoring the southern edge of Bank Street, brings TD Place stadium, year-round programming, a renovated Farmers Market, and a critical mass of retail that serves as both neighbourhood anchor and weekend destination. Its continued development has only added to Glebe's long-term real estate story.

Living in The Glebe

Three realities of Glebe real estate that every buyer and seller should understand.

Scarcity Drives Value

The Glebe is geographically constrained: bounded by the Rideau Canal, Lansdowne, the Queensway, and O'Connor Street. That finite supply combined with persistent demand means the neighbourhood has historically appreciated ahead of Ottawa averages and been resilient through market downturns.

Housing Character

The stock ranges from narrow semi-detached Victorian homes (typically $900K–$1.3M) through fully renovated detached properties with deep lots and coach houses (well over $2M). Condo options near the canal offer a lower entry point. Character is paramount: buyers come for the brickwork and the heritage feel, not square footage alone.

Transit & Walkability

The Glebe scores among Ottawa's highest walk scores. Carling and Glebe stations on the Confederation LRT line provide rapid transit connections. The 14 Rideau, 6 Beechwood, and multiple other routes run through the neighbourhood. For many residents, a car becomes optional.

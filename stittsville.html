The Neighbourhood

Small-Town Feel. Ottawa Access.

Stittsville is one of Ottawa's most distinct communities, a former independent village that retained its character after amalgamation into the city in 2001. Stittsville Main Street still functions like a genuine village strip, with local businesses, a hardware store, and community events that feel nothing like the generic suburban commercial corridors of newer developments nearby.

Residentially, the area has seen significant growth, with new construction expanding on both sides of Hazeldean Road. Buyers are drawn by the combination of newer homes at competitive prices, proximity to Kanata's employment base via Highway 417, and a community atmosphere that is harder to find in Ottawa's more developed suburbs.

The Christie Lake Conservation Area and Trans Canada Trail access give Stittsville an outdoor amenity offering that few Ottawa suburbs can match. For families who want a backyard and a weekend destination without leaving their address, this is a compelling proposition.

Living in Stittsville

Three defining characteristics of Stittsville real estate for buyers considering the far west corridor.

New Construction Value

Stittsville is one of Ottawa's most active new build markets. Buyers can choose from multiple active developments offering modern floor plans, builder warranties, and contemporary finishes, often at better price-per-square-foot than resale equivalents closer to the core.

Community Character

Stittsville Main Street gives the community something most new suburbs lack: an authentic commercial centre. Local events, a strong minor sports culture, and active community associations create a genuine sense of belonging that new residents consistently note as a deciding factor.

Highway Access

Highway 417 (the Queensway) is the primary commuting artery, putting downtown Ottawa within 25-30 minutes. Proximity to Kanata North's tech employment base is also a significant draw, many Stittsville residents work in Kanata and commute laterally rather than into the core.

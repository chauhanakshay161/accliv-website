Our Story

An Engineer's Approach
to Real Estate.

Accliv Group was built on a simple belief: the biggest financial decisions of your life deserve more rigor than instinct and a handshake. Here's the story behind the name, the method, and the person leading it.

The Name

Why We're Called Accliv.

The word comes from acclivity, an upward slope. It's a deliberate choice. Every client who works with us is mid-climb toward something: a first home, a better neighbourhood, a stronger portfolio, a new chapter.

Real estate isn't the destination. It's the foundation underneath it. The name is a reminder of what the work is actually for: progress, not paperwork.

That idea shapes everything about how Accliv Group operates: methodically, transparently, and always with the next chapter in mind, not just the closing date.

The Founder

Akshay Chauhan

Before real estate, Akshay's training was in engineering, a discipline built entirely around one principle: you don't guess, you calculate. You gather every variable, model the outcome, and remove uncertainty wherever it can be removed.

That mindset didn't get left behind when he moved into real estate. It became the operating system for it. Every recommendation Accliv Group makes, from what to offer to when to list to where to invest, is built on the same structured, data-first process used to solve engineering problems, applied instead to Ottawa's housing market.

"Most people make the largest purchase of their life on a gut feeling. I wanted to build something that replaces the gut feeling with a model, and still treats the client like a person, not a transaction."

Outside of client work, Akshay spends his time walking Ottawa's neighbourhoods block by block, tracking how streets change, what's being built, and where value is quietly shifting before it shows up in the headlines. It's research most agents skip. Accliv Group doesn't.

Akshay Chauhan
Founder & Principal Advisor, Ottawa, Ontario

The Method

An Engineering Process, Applied to Property

Every engagement follows the same disciplined sequence, borrowed directly from how technical problems get solved, not how real estate is traditionally sold.

01

Discover

We gather every requirement: budget, timeline, must-haves, deal-breakers, the way you'd scope a technical project before writing a line of code.

02

Analyze

Comparable sales, neighbourhood trends, pricing models. The data gets pulled apart before any property gets recommended.

03

Engineer the Strategy

A specific plan, covering pricing, timing, and negotiation approach, built for your situation, not copied from the last client.

04

Deliver & Validate

We execute, then check the result against the plan. If something didn't go as modelled, you hear about it directly. No spin.

What You Get
